Responsibilities

Data collection

As a research assistant working with Yuan in the Berkeley Early Learning Lab, your main responsibility is to help with data collection, including:
  • Recruiting participants from the Developmental Labs Recruitment System
  • Scheduling Zoom appointments with families who wish to participate
  • Conducting experiments with kids via Zoom
  • Saving video recordings to BELLBOX (Yuan's Studies)
  • Coding videos and saving results to Google Drive (yuan@bell)
  • Discussing how testing is going in weekly meetings as a group

Lab Business

Apart from working with Yuan, you also get to take part in lab-wide activities, including:
  • Attending weekly lab meetings on Wednesdays (11 a.m. - 12 p.m. PST)
  • Helping our lab manager with some administrative tasks (e.g., transferring participants from the old database, re-consenting families, etc.)

Additional Tasks

Depending on your interest, you can take on additional responsibilities that best utilize your skills or help your growth, such as:
  • Making suggestions about current studies (e.g., Unusual observations? Improve the task? Streamline the workflow?...)
  • Helping with the technical side of research (e.g., improving experiment websites, speeding up recruiting, writing tools for data coding and analysis, etc.)
  • Sharing cool studies/blog posts in cognitive science during weekly meetings
  • Asking Yuan about summer opportunities and additional resources (e.g., background literature, cognitive modeling, etc.)

Expectations

The Basics

For developmental research, the biggest bottleneck is getting enough kids to test. During the pandemic, recruiting has become even slower than before. Moreover, it is important that our participants have a satisfying experience so they may participate in the future. So as an RA, you will be expected to:
  • Send out ~20 recruiting emails per week
  • Coordinate with the families afterwards to ensure smooth participation
  • Make sure your Internet and Zoom work well before each appointment
  • Show up to each appointment on time and be well-prepared
  • Know experiment scripts by heart but can improvise accordingly
  • Make sure each experiment is properly recorded, stored, and coded
  • Communicate with Yuan and other team members should anything come up (e.g., you can't run an experiment, Internet problems, etc.)

What to Expect From Yuan

  • If you ask for additional tasks besides testing kids, I'll do my best to discuss with you and find something useful and interesting for you. However, if you don't ask, I won't add extra pressure on your shoulder.
  • I'm more than happy to provide a recommendation letter for your grad school/scholarship/industry/etc. application, provided that you have met the basic expectations while working with me and give me at least two weeks in advance.
  • Undergrad research should be fun and not a major stressor in your life. So if you feel stressed for any reason (e.g., heavy course load, being a different time zone, job hunting, etc.), please let me know and I'll adjust things for your accordingly.
